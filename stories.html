Kelvin Emmanuel Mkude

Growing up in Tandale, the largest slum in Dar es Salaam, Tanzania, Kelvin Mkude faced significant challenges due to his albinism. In a society rife with stigma, discrimination, and harmful superstitions such as the belief that the body parts of individuals with albinism can bring wealth and favour through witchcraft, Kelvin had to fight for his place. As a child, he took up karate to learn self-defense and protect himself from potential harm.

Kelvin's parents, determined to give him a better future, ensured he received an education. Their dedication paid off when Kelvin earned a bachelor's degree in aquaculture and aquatic science. This accomplishment likely makes him the first aquaculture officer with albinism in Tanzania. Despite the prejudice and misconceptions, he encountered throughout his academic journey, Kelvin persevered and excelled.

Motivated by his experiences and the desire to create change, Kelvin founded a non-governmental organization called Winning Nature in Charisma. This organization provides platforms for people with disabilities in Tanzania to develop and showcase their skills and talents, both professionally and personally.

Kelvin believes that individuals with disabilities possess untapped potential, innovative ideas, and solutions that have yet to be recognized. It takes just one person to believe in them, as Kelvin's parents did for him.

In its first two years, Winning Nature in Charisma has positively impacted over one hundred individuals with disabilities in Tanzania. The organization aims to triple this number by the end of its 2025 strategic plan.

Your contribution of $100 can significantly impact the lives of people with disabilities in Tanzania through Winning Nature in Charisma. By empowering more individuals and nurturing more success stories like Kelvin Mkude's, we can foster a society that values and supports the abilities of all its members.
